Thus, I hope to give back to my community while pursuing my dreams into my business pursuits. People often say that a certain place is like a second home to them, and more often than not, that place is somewhere they like to frequently visit or stay at. However, my second home is not a specific place, rather, it is a larger area and includes all the diverse groups of people living there. The second home I am referring to is my community.

In this community, there are public institutions that have been a large part of my childhood and made a profound impact on my daily life. In the beginning of my highschool years, I decided to give back to my community by volunteering at a public library, where I would encourage children to read many books and arrange events for them to participate in. Volunteering immediately became a pleasant activity as I would look forward to engaging with the children again. However, I noticed that there was a large number of homeless people that try to sit inside the library, only to be escorted outside. I thought about how it was unfair that the homeless were not able to stay at a place meant for public usage. Since they already did not have a home, how could they accept the community as their own if they are being rejected by it?

Hence, realizing that inequality and the mistreatment of homeless people is an overlooked issue in the community, I was inspired to volunteer at a homeless shelter. I worked with a group of people to serve food to the homeless. Everytime I handed a plate of food to them, they would thank me and show their appreciation with a warm smile, which made me happy since I was able to provide them with a place and environment where they will always be accepted. I now understand that my role in this community is to make others feel included. If I am able to call my community a second home, other people should be able to call their community a second -- or even first -- home as well.
